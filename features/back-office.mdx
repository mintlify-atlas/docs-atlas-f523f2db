Back Office Management in Ralph enables you to track and manage all IT equipment assigned to employees - from laptops and mobile phones to printers and accessories.

## Overview

The Back Office module provides:

- User equipment assignment and tracking
- Comprehensive asset lifecycle management
- Self-service equipment reporting
- Warehouse and inventory management
- Loan and return workflows
- Stock-taking and auditing tools

## Asset Types

### Back Office Assets

The primary asset type for user-assigned equipment:

- **Laptops** - Mobile computing devices
- **Desktops** - Workstations and PCs
- **Monitors** - Displays and screens
- **Printers** - Network and local printers
- **Mobile Phones** - Smartphones and cellular devices
- **Tablets** - iPads and Android tablets
- **Accessories** - Keyboards, mice, docking stations, headsets

### Asset Status

Back office assets support a comprehensive range of statuses:

**Active States**
- `new` - Newly purchased, not yet deployed
- `in use` - Actively assigned to a user
- `in use team` - Shared team equipment
- `in use test` - Test/QA equipment
- `free` - Available for assignment
- `reserved` - Reserved for specific user or project

**Process States**
- `in progress` - Being prepared for deployment
- `loan` - Temporarily loaned to user
- `loan in progress` - Loan being processed
- `return in progress` - Being returned from user
- `in service` - Under repair or maintenance

**Inactive States**
- `damaged` - Broken or non-functional
- `liquidated` - Disposed or sold
- `quarantine` - Quarantined for security or health reasons
- `taken for disposal` - Marked for disposal

<Note>
Status names can include suffixes like "(mgmt excluded)" or "(update excluded)" to control which assets appear in management systems or receive automated updates.
</Note>

## User Assignment

### Assigning Equipment to Users

Assets can be assigned to individual users:

1. **Select Asset** - Open the asset detail page
2. **Choose User** - Select from the user directory
3. **Set Status** - Update to "in use" or appropriate status
4. **Add Notes** - Document assignment reason or configuration

### Assignment History

Ralph maintains complete assignment history:

- Previous users and owners
- Assignment dates and durations
- Status changes over time
- Transition reasons and notes

### User Equipment View

Users can access "My Equipment" to see:

- All assets currently assigned to them
- Asset details and specifications
- Support contact information
- Stock-taking confirmation interface

## Office Infrastructure

Organize back office assets by physical location:

- **Office Locations** - Buildings and office sites
- **Departments** - Organizational units
- **Floor/Room** - Detailed physical location
- **Assignment Tracking** - Equipment by location

This helps with:
- Physical inventory audits
- Equipment retrieval
- Location-based reporting
- Site-specific policies

## Warehouses

### Warehouse Management

Store and track assets before deployment:

- **Multiple Warehouses** - Support for distributed storage
- **Stock Levels** - Current inventory counts
- **Stock-Taking** - Enable periodic audits
- **External Depot ID** - Integration with external systems

### Stock-Taking Process

Streamline physical inventory verification:

1. **Enable Stock-Taking** - Check box in warehouse admin
2. **User Confirmation** - Users verify their equipment via "My Equipment"
3. **Inventory Tags** - System records confirmation with timestamps
4. **Audit Reports** - Export verification results
5. **Disable When Complete** - Uncheck to end the process

<Tip>
Enable stock-taking per warehouse or per region to target specific groups of assets. This allows rolling audits without disrupting the entire organization.
</Tip>

## Mobile Devices

### IMEI Tracking

Mobile phones include specialized fields:

- **IMEI** - International Mobile Equipment Identity
- **Validation** - Automatic IMEI format validation
- **SIM Card** - Associated SIM card tracking
- **Carrier** - Mobile carrier information

<Note>
Ralph validates IMEI numbers against both pre-2003 and post-2003 formats to ensure data accuracy.
</Note>

## Equipment Loans

### Loan Workflow

Manage temporary equipment assignments:

1. **Loan Request** - Create loan with start/end dates
2. **Status Change** - Set to "loan" or "loan in progress"
3. **User Assignment** - Assign to borrower
4. **Return Process** - Set to "return in progress"
5. **Completion** - Return to warehouse or reassign

### Tracking

- Loan duration and expected return date
- Borrower information
- Loan purpose and notes
- Overdue loan alerts

## Asset Models

### Back Office Models

When creating asset models for back office equipment:

1. **Set Type** - Choose "Back Office" as model type
2. **Category** - Select appropriate category (laptop, phone, printer, etc.)
3. **Manufacturer** - Link to manufacturer database
4. **Specifications** - Add technical details

<Tip>
Create models for your standard employee laptop configurations (e.g., "Developer Laptop 2024", "Sales Laptop Standard"). This makes bulk purchasing and deployment much easier.
</Tip>

## Transitions and Workflows

Automate common back office processes:

### Built-in Transitions

- **Deploy to User** - Change status from warehouse to in use
- **Return from User** - Return equipment to warehouse
- **Send for Repair** - Mark as in service
- **Loan Equipment** - Process temporary loan
- **Liquidate** - Mark for disposal or sale

### Custom Workflows

Create custom transitions for your organization:

- New hire equipment provisioning
- Employee departure asset collection
- Equipment refresh cycles
- Repair and maintenance workflows

See [Workflows & Transitions](/features/workflows) for detailed configuration.

## Best Practices

<Tip>
**Standardize Models**: Maintain a catalog of standard employee equipment configurations. This simplifies procurement, deployment, and support.
</Tip>

<Tip>
**Document Assignments**: Always update asset status and user assignment together. This keeps your inventory accurate and makes reporting reliable.
</Tip>

<Tip>
**Regular Stock-Taking**: Schedule periodic stock-taking exercises (quarterly or semi-annually) to maintain inventory accuracy and identify missing equipment.
</Tip>

<Note>
Use the "My Equipment" self-service feature to reduce administrative overhead during stock-taking. Users can confirm their equipment directly, saving IT staff significant time.
</Note>
